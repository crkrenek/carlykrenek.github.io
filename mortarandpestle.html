Mortar & Pestle | July-August 2021

Description

The client, Mortar & Pestle, requested an initial website design for their Vermont based cafe/bakery.

My Roles

  • Research
  • User Personas
  • Use Case & Storyboard
  • Wireframe
  • UX/UI Design Mockup

Tools Used

  • Balsamiq
  • Adobe Photoshop

The Challenge

Design a new website for the cafe/bakery, Mortar & Pestle, that will help attract new customers and increase sales by offering an online ordering experience.

Phase 1: Define & Research

In place of an initial discovery meeting, I collected information about the company, their needs, and goals via a client survey created with Google Forms. The responses I received informed my competitive analysis and allowed me to determine a user base for Mortar & Pestle.

Personas

Four distinct personas were created to build empathy with visitors of the site and identify their potential user goals and needs.

Rachel Harris | 28

Digital Marketing Specialist

Rachel loves a sweet treat and cup of coffee, especially when working on projects. She's also a people person and likes to treat her coworkers every once in a while. Most days Rachel is dropping in on her way to work to pick up breakfast for herself, but sometimes she likes to order coffee and goodies for the office. On occasion, she will order lunch from the office and walk over to pick it up. On the weekends, Rachel likes ot head to the cafe to catch up with friends and has been there a time or two for a date. Often times when she's using the site, she's looking to find a menu, quick online ordering, a simple catering form, and/or a rewards program or current specials.

Use Cases

A basic use case was written for each of the unique personas in order for me to determine what tasks users will likely perform on the site and what potential alternative routes they might take.

Use Case

Rachel’s goal is to request catering information for a brunch she would like to host for her team.

Actor

Rachel Harris

Basic Flow

The user will go to the Catering & Custom Orders link in the navigation menu. The page will display an inquiry form that the user will fill out with details about their requested order. When the user is done filling out the form they will click “Submit”. The page will then display a confirmation that their message was sent along with a "thank you" message from Mortar & Pestle. The user will exit the site.

High Fidelity Mockup

Photoshop was my tool of choice, used to create high fidelity wireframe mockups for my "Rachel Harris" persona and use case scenario.

Scene 1

Rachel accesses the Mortar & Pestle home page looking for catering information. She finds the Catering & Custom Orders link in the navigation menu and clicks on it. She is taken to the Catering & Custom Orders page.

Scene 2

Rachel doesn't know what menu items are offered for catering, so she clicks on the Catering Menu button in order to determine what items she is interested in ordering for the brunch training. She is taken to the Catering Menu page.

Scene 3

Rachel spends some time viewing the catering menu and decides she would like to inquire about pricing and availability. She sees the Inquire Now call-to-action button and clicks on it. She is taken to the Catering Inquiry form page.

Scene 4

Rachel completes the inquiry form by inputting her name, email, phone number, and selecting the day of her training. In the message box, she requests a quote for the Self-Serve Medley and Coffee Box for 7 people. She then clicks submit and a confirmation message is displayed.
